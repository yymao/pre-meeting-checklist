Checklist for the LSST DESC Sprint Week (Edinburgh 2018)

Hereâ€™s a few useful things you can do to prepare yourself for the upcoming Sprint Week in Edinburgh!

Bookmark the Sprint Week Confluence page, on both your laptop and phone. Make sure you remember your Confluence password.
Add your name and departure time to the Participants table on the Sprint Week page.
Add your hack/sprint ideas to the Sprints and Hacks table on the Sprint Week page! Remember, not all hacks and sprints have to be carried out, so be bold when adding ideas!
Add breakout session requests/ideas to the Breakout Sessions table on the Sprint Week page.
Join the Slack channel #desc-hack-sprint to hear announcements and to chat with sprint participants (both in-person and virtual).
You should have received an email from Joe Zuntz, with the subject line "Edinburgh Sprint Week Details", in which you can find logistical details about the Sprint Week. Keep that email handy. If you have any questions about logistics, you can contact Joe Zuntz.
Make sure you have a NERSC account and remember your password (nope, a SSH key is not enough)! Need to apply for one? Forget your password? Questions? Ask them in the #desc-nersc Slack channel. Also, make sure you are in the "lsst" group. If not, contact Richard Dubois or Debbie Bard to request to be added to the "lsst" group.
If you plan to use DC2 data products, make sure you have DESC jupyter kernels installed. You can also follow the instruction on this step-by-step guide to familiarize yourself with DC2 data products. Also check out and bookmark the DC2 Data Product Overview page.
Remind yourself of our Code of Conduct and the Virtual Meeting Best Practices. Note that Code of Conduct applies to virtual participants too and many of the Best Practices apply to in-person participants as well!
Want to be free from technical difficulties? Check out our Tech Cheat Sheet and bookmark it!
If you'd like to carpool or to share accommodation with other participants, you can find links to editable Google Sheets on the top blue banner of the Sprint Week Confluence page.
If you use social media professionally, please use the #lsstdesc hashtag when reporting highlights from the meeting!
