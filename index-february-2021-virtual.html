Checklist for the February 2021 LSST DESC Virtual Collaboration Meeting

Here are a few useful things you can do to prepare yourself for our second Virtual Collaboration Meeting!

Access meeting resources

Check out and bookmark the Collaboration Meeting confluence page (on all the devices that you plan to use). Make sure you remember your Confluence password.
Check out the event calendar on the Meeting Schedule page. You can subscribe to the calendar to keep up to date on all the events for the week.
Check out the logistics and guidelines page for details on e.g., participation guidelines, Slack channels, Zoom and Wonder access.
Remind yourself of our Code of Conduct which applies to all participants.

Prepare for engaging during the meeting

Ensure that your Zoom and Slack apps are up-to-date.
Sign in and explore the social interaction tool (use this link then scroll down to the "Social Interactions with Wonder" and click to explore)
  • A brief overview of Wonder will be presented at Monday's plenary but you are welcome to view the slides in advance.
Add your preferred pronouns to any apps in use, e.g., as <First-Name> <Last-Name> (<pronoun>). Wondering why is this important? See here.
  • For Zoom, you can just add your pronouns to your name. Note that you can rename yourself by clicking on the three dots in your square and clicking "Rename".
  • For Slack, you can add your pronouns to your profile by 1) clicking on your name, 2) clicking the "Edit Profile" button, and 3) filling out/updating the "Preferred Pronouns" entry.
Consider setting your Slack status as active if you are available for synchronous activities.
Join the following Slack channels:
  • #desc-collab-meeting to hear announcements and daily briefings.
  • #desc-collab-meeting-hotline to report any technical problems that may arise during the meeting.

Computing resources etc.

If you plan to join an analysis project during the meeting, make sure you have a NERSC account and remember your password (nope, a SSH key is not enough) and have 2-step verification (MFA) set up!
  • Need to apply for one?
  • Forgot your password?
  • Questions? Ask them in the #desc-nersc Slack channel.
Also, make sure you are in the "lsst" group. If not, contact Richard Dubois (slack: @richard), Heather Kelly (slack: @heather999) or Debbie Bard (slack: @djbard) to request to be added to the "lsst" group.
Follow the instructions to install DESC jupyter kernels. You will need them to access e.g., DC2 data products or to use DESC software during the Meeting.
If you have not used any DC2 data tutorial notebooks before, check out the instructions on this step-by-step guide. Also, bookmark the DC2 Data Product Overview page and the DC2-analysis GitHub repo for easy access. Questions? Ask them in the #desc-dc2-users Slack channel.

Social media

If you use social media professionally, please use the #lsstdesc hashtag when reporting highlights from the meeting! Remember the Code of Conduct mentioned above!
